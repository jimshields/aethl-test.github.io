Chronic wounds affect far more than skin.

Patients often experience prolonged pain, reduced mobility, and social isolation, while families and caregivers shoulder the burden of long‑term care. Non‑healing wounds quietly erode independence, dignity, and quality of life—sometimes for years.

The system treats closure, not recovery

When underlying skin function is not restored, wounds are more likely to invite infection, rehospitalization, and life‑altering complications. Without approaches that prioritize functional healing, patients remain trapped in cycles of incomplete recovery.
